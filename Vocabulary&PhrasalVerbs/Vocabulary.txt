Amass.
    Amass (Verb) - to collect or gather - to get a large amount of something, especially money or information, by collecting it over a long period.
        Synonyms:
            assemble, accumulate.
        For Example:
            She has amassed a huge fortune from her novels.
            She is amassing a lot of data for her thesis.

Acrimonious.
    Acrimonious (Adjective) - full of anger, arguments and bad feeling - angry and bitter - acid.
    Acrimonious word have "Acri" which means Bitter.
        For Example:
            An acrimonious dispute about wages.
            Their marriage ended 8 years ago in an acrimonious divorce.

Anomaly.
    Anomaly (Noun) - irregularity - something that deviates from what is standard, usual and expected.
        Synonyms:
            abnormality, irregularity, inconsistency, incongruity, deviation
        For Example:
            There are a number of anomalies in the present system.
        Hind:
            anomaly - abnormality.

Aptitude.
    Aptitude (Noun) - a natural ability, or skill (natural).
        Synonyms:
            talent, fitness, gift, skill, ability.
        For Example:
            My son has no aptitude for sport.
    
Alimony.
    Alimony (Noun) - payment to divorced wife.
        Synonyms:
            maintenance, support, provision.
        For Example:
            He is said to have paid 10$ alimony to his first wife.
        Hint:
            Ali+mony = all I need is money, not my husband.

Atone.
    Atone (Verb) - to do something that shows that you are sorry for something bad you did or make amends for.
        Synonyms:
            Compensate
        For Example:
            You must atone for the injustice you have done to innocent people.

Ambiguous.
    Ambiguous (Adjective) - expressing more than one possible meaning sometimes intentionally.
    Ambi = both.
        Synonyms:
            Equivocal, ambivalent
        For Example:
            His reply to my question was somewhat ambiguous.
            The wording of the agreement is ambiguous.

Abjure.
    Abjure (Verb) - publicly renounce or give up - a belief, cause, claim.
        For Example:
            you must abjure the pursuit of sinful acts.
        Hint:
            abjure, think it like "injure". When you are injure you give up.

Adjure.
    Adjure (Verb) - ask for or request - command solemnly.
        For Example:
            I adjure you to tell me the truth.
            The judge adjured him to answer the question truthfully.
        Hint:
            Ad + Jure = Jury always commands.
    
Allege.
    Allege (Verb) - claim or assert that someone has done something illegal or wrong, without giving proof.
        Synonyms:
            state or proclaim.
        For Example:
            She alleged that she has been molested.

Analogy.
    Analogy (Noun) - drawing a comparison in order to show some similarity in some respect., typically for the purpose of explanation and clarification.
        For Example:
            He drew an analogy between the brain and a vast computer.
