Tenses.

NOTE.
Only when the subject do something in a sentence is the sentence of tense.
Otherwise, it's a simple sentence where subject doesn't do anything.
    For Example:
        I play.
        I am playing.
        I will play.

Tenses has 3 times.
    1. Past.
    2. Present.
    3. Future.

Each time has 4 Categories.
    1. Indifinite.
    2. Continuous.
    3. Perfect.
    4. Perfect Continuous.

Present Indifinite.
Identity: (Ta, Te, Te, etc... optionally hai, hain, hon, hun, etc...).
    Affirmative: Subject + Verb 1st Form + (s, es - 3rd person singular) + Object.
    Negative: Subject + do/does + not + Verb 1st form + Object.
    Interrogative: <WH> + do/does + Subject + Verb 1st form + Object?
    Negative Interrogative: <WH> + do/does + Subject + not + Verb 1st form + Object?
    Special Case:
        Interrogative: <WH-Subject> + Verb 1st Form + (s, es - 3rd person singular) + Object?
        Negative Interrogative: <WH-Subject> + do/does + not + Verb 1st Form + Object?

NOTE:
    We use 's', 'es' only in affirmatives.

Where to put s/es?
    When subject is 3rd person singular.
    As hint:
    -> When a word ends with 'o', 'x', 'ss', 'sh', we put 'es'.
        Go -> Goes.
        Mix -> Mixes.
        Miss -> Misses.
        Wish -> Wishes.
    -> When before 'y' there is a vowel, we put 's'.
        Play -> Plays.
        Enjoy -> Enjoys.
    -> When before 'y' there is no vowel, we remove 'y' and put 'ies'.
        Try -> Tries.
        Apply -> Applies.

Present Continuous Tense.
Identity: (Raha hai, rhi hai, rahe hain, rahi hai, etc...).
    Affirmative: Subject + is/am/are + Verb 1st form + ing + Object.
    Negative: Subject + is/am/are + not + Verb 1st form + ing + object.
    Interrogative: <WH> + is/am/are + Subject + Verb 1st form + ing + object?
    Negative Interrogative: <WH> + is/am/are + Subject + not + Verb 1st form + ing + object?
    Special Case:
        Interrogative: <Wh-Subject> + is/am/are + Verb 1st form + ing + Object?
        Negative Interrogative: <Wh-Subject> + is/am/are + not + Verb 1st form + ing + Object?

NOTE:
Is -> 3rd person singular.
Am -> First person singular.
Are -> All Plurals.

Practice:
Pooja masale pees rhi hai.
    Pooja is grinding the spices.
Pooja masale nhi pees rhi hai.
    Pooja is not grinding the spices.
Kia Pooja masale pees rhi hai?
    Is Pooja grinding the spices?
Pooja masale kiun pees rhi hai?
    Why is Pooja grinding the spices?
Pooja masale kahan pees rhi hai?
    Where is Pooja grinding the spices?
Pooja masale kiske sath pees rhi hai?
    With whom is Pooja grinding the spices?
Pooja masale kis larki ke sath pees rhi hai?
    With which girl is Pooja grinding the spices?
Pooja kia pees rhi hai?
    What is Pooja grinding?
Pooja or Archana kia pees rhe hain?
    What are Pooja and Archana grinding?
NOTE: Pooja and Archana is subject. And we treat as single subject. Not two. But the subject is plural.
Woh apni success ka credit kise de raha hai?
    Whom is he giving the credit of his success?
Kis larki ka bhai yahan aa raha hai?
    Which girl's brother is comming here?
    Which girl's brother is comming over here?
Yeh pen kon kharid raha hai?
    Who is buying this pen?
Yeh pen kon kharid rahe hain?
    Who are buying this pen?
Mai aesa kiu soch raha hu?
    Why am I thinking so?
    Why am I thinking like that?
    Why am I thinking like this?
Tum aesa keh kar mera dil dukha rhy ho?
    You're hurting me by saying this.
    You're hurting me by telling this.
Koi mujhe yaad kar raha hai.
    Someone is missing me.

Present Perfect Tense.
Identity: (chuka hai, chuki hai, chuke hain, etc... lia hai, diya hai,,, means subject ne kuch kar diya hai).
    Affirmative: Subject + has/have + Verb 3rd form + Object.
    Negative: Subject + has/have + not + Verb 3rd form + Object.
    Interrogative: <WH> + has/have + Subject + Verb 3rd form + Object?
    Negative Interrogative: <WH> + has/have + Subject + not + Verb 3rd form + Object?
    Special Case:
        Interrogative: <WH-Subject> + has/have + Verb 3rd form + Object?
        Negative Interrogative: <WH-Subject> + has/have + not + Verb 3rd form + Object?

NOTE:
has -> 3rd person singular.
have -> rest all.

NOTE:
For perfect tense, we use verb 3rd form, for past, present, and future.

Present Perfect Continuous Tense.
Identity: (raha hoon, rahe hain, etc... (continuous) but with time (since/for)).
    Affirmative: Subject + has/have + been + Verb 1st form + ing + Object + since/for + Time.
    Negative: Subject + has/have + not + been + Verb 1st form + ing + Object + since/for + Time.
    Interrogative: <Wh> + has/have + Subject + been + Verb 1st form + ing + Object + since/for + Time.
    Negative Interrogative: <Wh> + has/have + Subject + not + been + Verb 1st form + ing + Object + since/for + Time.
    Special Case:
        Interrogative: <WH-Subject> + has/have + been + Verb 1st form + ing + Object + since/for + Time.
        Negative Interrogative: <WH-Subject> + has/have + not + been + Verb 1st form + ing + Object + since/for + Time.

Practice:

Past Indefinite Tense.
Identity: (Krta tha, krte thy, tha, thi, thy, lia, diya, di, li, etc...).
    Affirmative: Subject + Verb 2nd form + Object.
    Negative: Subject + did + not + Verb 1st form + Object.
    Interrogative: <WH> + did + Subject + Verb 1st form + Object?
    Negative Interrogative: <WH> + did + Subject + not + Verb 1st form + Object?
    Special Case:
        Interrogative: <Wh-Subject> + Verb 2nd form + Object?
        Negative Interrogative: <WH-Subject> + did + not + Verb 1st form + Object?

NOTE.
It Past Indefinite is the only tense where we use 2nd form of verb in affirmative sentence.
And in negative sentence, we use 'did' but not in affirmative.
We use 'did' as auxaliry verb in all cases, not other forms of did.
But yeah, we can use as main verb.

With (Tha, They, Thi, ... Ta, Te, Ti, etc...).
-> Action Verbs (Play, Dance, Cook, etc...).
    -> Used to/ Would.
-> Non-Action Verbs (Think, Like, Want, Believe, etc...).
    -> Past Indifinte Tense.
    -> Used to/ Would.

NOTE.
We use Verb 1st form when using Used to in this case.
When making negative of 'used to' we use 'did not use to'.

Practice:
Mai SJ ko pasand krta tha.
    I liked SJ.
    I used to like SJ.
Hum is cheez par vishwas krte they.
    We believed in this thing.
    We used to believe in this thing.
    -> we use 'in' after believe.
Mai SJ ko pasand nhi krta tha.
    I did not like SJ.
    I did not use to like SJ.
    -> When we make negative using used to, we put did not, and use 'use to' instead of 'used to'.
Kia mai SJ ko pasand krta tha?
    Did I like SJ?
    Did I use to like SJ?
Kia mai SJ ko pasand nhi krta tha?
    Did I not like SJ?
    Did I not use to like SJ?
Mai uske sath khelta tha.
    I used to play with him.
Hum aksar us se baat krte thy.
    We used to often talk to him.

NOTE.
When we talk about a specific time, date in the past.
We can make sentence with used to, but also with past indefinite tense.

Practice:
Mai 2000 me uske sath khelta tha.
    I played with him in 2000.
    I used to play with him in 2000.
Hum un dino us se baat krte thy.
    We used to often talk to him those days.
    We talked to him those days.

    We used to meet every Sunday those days.
    We met every Sunday those days.

NOTE.
When we make sentence with 'used to' and we have to tell lines and lines. Like in a sequence, we use 'used to' for first and for next and next and next... 'would'.
We use Verb 1st form with 'would' as well.

Practice:
Woh chawal banata tha, or mai roti banata tha.
    He used to cook rice, and I would cook roti.
Hum wahan milty thy, Fir hum school jate thy, Fir hum khelty thy.
    We used to meet there. Then, We would go to school. Then, We would play.

Past Continuous Tense.
(Raha tha, Rahi thi, Rahy the, etc...).
    Affirmative: Subject + was/were + Verb 1st form + ing + Object.
    Negative: Subject + was/were + not + Verb 1st form + ing + Object.
    Interrogative: <WH> + was/were + Subject + Verb 1st form + ing + Object?
    Negative Interrogative: <Wh> + was/were + Subject + not + Verb 1st form + ing + Object?
    Special Case:
        Interrogative: <WH-Subject> + was/were + Verb 1st form + ing + Object?
        Negative Interrogative: <WH-Subject> + was/were + not + Verb 1st form + ing + Object?

Note.
We use 'was' with singular subject.
We use 'were' with plural subject.

Practice.
Woh aek santare ko cheel rahi thi.
    She was peeling an orange.
    She was peeling off an orange.
Woh aek santare ko nhi cheel rahi thi.
    She was not peeling an orange.
    She was not peeling off an orange.
Kia woh aek santare ko cheel rahi thi?
    Was she peeling an orange?
    Was she peeling off an orange?
Kia woh aek santara nhi cheez rahi thi?
    Was she not peeling an orange?
    Was she not peeling off an orange?
Woh kia cheel rahi thi?
    What was she peeling?
    What was she peeling off?
Mai kise bta raha tha?
    Whom was I telling?
Hum office se nikal rahe thy.
    We were leaving the office.
    -> We were leaving from the office. (wrong).
        -> we don't put any preposition when using leave in that context.
Hum office k liye nikal rahe thy.
    We were leaving for the office.
    -> We were leaving for the office. (correct).
        -> we add 'for' because now the context is of going.
Kon kon has rahe thy?
    Who all were laughing?
Mujh par kon kon has rahe thy?
    Who all were laughing at me?
    -> we use 'at' with laughing.
Mujh par class me kon kon has rahe thy?
    Who all were laughing at me in the class?
Tum use kiun dosh de rahe thy?
    Why you were blaming him?
    -> 'you' is treated plural everytime.
Kiski behan wahan aa rahi thi?
    Whose sister was comming there?
    Whose sister was comming over there?
